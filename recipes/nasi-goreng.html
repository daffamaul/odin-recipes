Nasi Goreng

Ingredients

  • Cooked rice (preferably day-old rice)
  • Cooking oil (commonly vegetable oil)
  • Shallots or onions, finely chopped
  • Garlic, minced
  • Chilies, finely chopped (optional for spice)
  • Protein such as chicken, shrimp, or tofu, diced or sliced
  • Vegetables like carrots, peas, bell peppers, and cabbage, thinly sliced
  • Kecap manis (sweet soy sauce), or a mixture of soy sauce and brown sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional garnishes: fried shallots, sliced cucumber, tomato wedges, fried egg, and fresh cilantro or spring onions.

Steps

  1. Prepare Ingredients: Gather and prepare all the ingredients needed for your nasi goreng. Chop vegetables, dice protein, mince garlic, etc.
  2. Cook Rice: If you don't already have cooked rice, cook it according to package instructions. It's best to use day-old rice for nasi goreng, as it tends to fry up better without becoming mushy.
  3. Heat Oil: Heat a wok or large frying pan over medium-high heat. Add cooking oil and allow it to heat up.
  4. Sauté Aromatics: Add finely chopped shallots or onions to the hot oil and sauté until they become translucent. Then add minced garlic and chopped chilies if you're using them, and sauté for another minute until fragrant.
  5. Add Protein: Add your chosen protein (chicken, shrimp, tofu, etc.) to the pan. Stir-fry until the protein is cooked through.
  6. Add Vegetables: Add thinly sliced vegetables to the pan. Common choices include carrots, peas, bell peppers, and cabbage. Stir-fry for a few minutes until the vegetables are slightly softened but still crisp.
  7. Season with Sauces: Pour in kecap manis (sweet soy sauce) or a mixture of soy sauce and brown sugar for sweetness. Adjust the amount according to your taste preferences. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Add Rice: Add the cooked rice to the pan. Use a spatula to break up any clumps and evenly distribute the rice with the other ingredients. Stir-fry everything together until the rice is heated through and well-coated with the sauce.
  9. Taste and Adjust: Taste the nasi goreng and adjust the seasoning if needed. You can add more soy sauce, kecap manis, or spices according to your preference.
  10. Serve: Once everything is cooked and seasoned to your liking, transfer the nasi goreng to serving plates or bowls. Garnish with optional toppings like fried shallots, sliced cucumber, tomato wedges, a fried egg, and fresh cilantro or spring onions.
  11. Enjoy: Serve your nasi goreng hot and enjoy it as a delicious and satisfying meal!
